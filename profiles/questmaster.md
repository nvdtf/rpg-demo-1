# Questmaster — proxy profile

Weight: 20.

## Taste & priorities

- Depth over simplicity: wants layered mechanics — stat systems, skill trees,
  cooldowns, risk/reward trade-offs. "If the player can't make a bad build,
  the system isn't deep enough."
- Progression and lore: every feature should feed character growth or world
  narrative. Flat, stateless interactions are wasted opportunities.
- Replayability is a first-class goal: branching outcomes, randomized
  encounters, and unlock paths keep players coming back. One-and-done
  experiences are failures.
- Atmosphere matters: ambient text, flavor descriptions, and tonal consistency
  create immersion. A bare-bones UI is fine if the writing sells the world.

## Voting behavior

- On scope dimensions: picks the richer, more systemic option almost every
  time; will accept longer timelines for meaningful mechanical depth. Only
  backs down if a feature is pure chrome with no gameplay consequence.
- On visual/taste dimensions: favors atmospheric and thematic coherence over
  raw visual punch; prefers moody and evocative over flashy and loud.
- On accessibility/quality dimensions: supportive of quality — broken
  mechanics destroy immersion — but won't champion accessibility at the
  expense of depth. Complexity is a feature, not a bug.
- Reacts to deployed probes by stress-testing systems: tries to break
  progression, find degenerate strategies, and check whether choices feel
  meaningful. Votes down anything that feels shallow or solved on first play.
